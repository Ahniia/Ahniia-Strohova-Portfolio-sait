Rhema

Overview

This is a website redesign for a modern church from Linz. They already had a website, but wanted to update it without changing the existing site structure . In addition, a logo and color map were provided by the client. The design needed to be cozy, friendly, but also a little conservative.

My Role

Sole UX/UI Designer
(colors and logo were provided by the customer)

Duration

1 month

Tools

Figma, Colour Contrast Analyser,  Adobe Illustrator, Excel, UsabilityHub, Paper & Pencil

Working moments

Old design

The problems of the old design

  • Old site style
  • The site style doesn’t match the organization style
  • Lots of different colors that focus attention on themselves rather than on the page content
  • Bright backgrounds overlapping the content of the photos.White text is hard to read against a light bright background, and plus the background photo, it’s uncomfortable to read

Previous Next

New design

How have the problems been solved

  • Modern site style
  • Menu is less wide, making the site more adaptive for devices of different widths
  • The photo does not overlap the background
  • Text has sufficient contrast to the background (using Colour Contrast Analyser), the background is monochrome. The text is easy to read
  • Decorative elements create a feeling of coziness, but do not distract from reading the information
  • The distances between the elements of the site (text, photos, images, frames) are built on a grid and comply with design standards, which simplifies the user ‘s perception
